## Scope

Marketing strategy, brand positioning, and digital presence:

- brand positioning and value proposition;
- target audience, occasion, and use-case signals;
- campaign themes, promotions, events, partnerships, and PR;
- website, search, social, public ads, and platform presence;
- delivery, booking, and discovery-platform merchandising;
- photos, descriptions, messaging, ratings display, and offer framing;
- competitor brand momentum and visible marketing activity.

## Evidence To Seek

- Venue websites, landing pages, booking and ordering flows.
- Public social profiles, content themes, and recent post cadence when accessible.
- Public ad libraries when accessible.
- Search result composition for the venue and key competitors.
- Delivery, reservation, and discovery-platform listings for photos, copy, rankings, promotions, and completeness.
- Local launch posts, event posts, influencer/creator posts, partnerships, PR, and press mentions when relevant.

## Boundaries

- Do not assume social profiles, ad libraries, or platform rankings are accessible.
- Do not infer private marketing strategy or exact ad spend from public signals.
- Do not analyze item-level menu prices. Use `menu_pricing`.
- Do not analyze review sentiment. Use `guest_intelligence` or `review_analyst`.

## Output Notes

Compare visible strategy, positioning, and execution against the competitive set. Use clear labels such as active, dormant, differentiated, generic, premium, value-led, incomplete, strong, weak, or unavailable, with evidence.
