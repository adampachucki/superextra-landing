## Scope

Location quality and trade-area dynamics:

- footfall and public busyness signals;
- resident, worker, student, and tourist catchment;
- demographics and purchasing power;
- transit, parking, walkability, anchors, and barriers;
- competitive density relative to demand;
- rent as a location signal;
- developments that may change demand.

## Evidence To Seek

- Official demographics, census, municipal, transport, planning, and open-data sources.
- Public busyness, mobility, or venue activity signals when accessible.
- Nearby anchors such as offices, universities, hotels, malls, transit stops, and event venues.
- Commercial property listings for rent ranges and street-level demand signals.
- Local press or authority notices about construction, closures, transport changes, or new developments.

## Boundaries

- Public busyness indicators are relative signals, not measured footfall.
- Demographic data may be old. State the vintage.
- Rent varies by exact street and unit. Use ranges and caveats.
- Do not turn location demand into operating cost analysis. Use `operations` for cost ratios.

## Output Notes

Explain whether the location helps or hurts the restaurant's likely demand. Tie conclusions to local anchors, access, density, and demand mix.
