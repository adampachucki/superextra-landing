# Superextra — Copy Guidelines & Ad Collection

Living doc for marketing copy voice and the running ad set. Built from the
landing-page voice and refined in conversation. Update as the voice evolves.

---

## Product, in one breath

Superextra is the **AI consultant for every restaurant** — an external
market-intelligence layer that synthesizes competitor, pricing, guest,
delivery, and demand signals into operator-ready answers: where to open, what
to serve, how to price, when to advertise, and what's shifting around them.

- **Audience:** restaurant operators (owners, chains, decision-makers)
- **First market:** US
- **Anchor lines:** _"AI consultant for every restaurant."_ · _"Super intelligence. Extra advantage."_

---

## Voice principles

1. **Every part connects.** Each line earns its place and strengthens the one
   before. A coherent whole — problem → what Superextra does → a payoff that
   _follows from_ it. Nothing bolted on.
2. **Open with something instantly graspable.** Concrete nouns, not
   abstractions. A vague first line doesn't get read.
3. **Always land an outcome.** Knowing → doing → result: more guests, better
   margins, the right location, moving before competitors. Say what clarity buys.
4. **Concrete over abstract.** Name the decisions (open, serve, price,
   advertise) and the signals (competitors, reviews, demand).
5. **AI is fair game.** "AI consultant" is clear and credible — don't bury it.
6. **"You" is welcome.** Direct, warm, plain.
7. **Calm, declarative, punchy.** Short sentences, one idea per line, restraint.
   It has to sound well read aloud.
8. **Spread the territory.** Don't lean on one theme across the set — we caught
   pricing showing up in three ads and trimmed it. Each ad owns distinct ground.

**Never:** exclamation marks, emoji, hype words (supercharge, unlock,
revolutionary), jargon (leverage, holistic), fake urgency. The only real
urgency is _limited early access_.

**Name:** always **Superextra** — never SuperExtra, Super Extra, or SUPEREXTRA.

## Format & CTA notes

**Meta ad structure**

- Primary text: 1–2 tight sentences; front-load the first ~125 characters.
- Headline: ≤ ~40 characters.
- Description (optional): ~30 characters.

**CTA buttons are a fixed Meta dropdown — not free text.**

- **Default:** Get Started (forward, low-friction).
- Demo-accurate alternatives: Book Now · Request Time.
- Exclusivity angle: Apply Now (plays to "limited early access").
- Avoid: Sign Up, Learn More — weak, no value.

## Ad copy collection

Four ads, four distinct territories: demand shifts (A), the big decisions (B),
competitors (C), pricing (D).

### A · Why is it happening? _(demand / what changed)_

> Sales drop and no one can say why. Superextra shows what changed around you — find the fix before it sticks.

- **Headline:** Know what changed — and why
- **CTA:** Get Started

### B · The big decisions _(location / menu / marketing)_

> Where to open. What to serve. When to advertise. Superextra answers the decisions that make or break a restaurant — with live data from the market around you.

- **Headline:** AI consultant for every restaurant
- **CTA:** Get Started

### C · Beat the competitor _(competitor intel)_

> See how the restaurants near you advertise, what their guests love, and where they're winning. Superextra keeps you one step ahead of the competition next door.

- **Headline:** Beat the restaurant next door
- **CTA:** Get Started

### D · Pricing _(menu pricing)_

> See every competitor's prices. Set yours to win. Superextra benchmarks your whole menu against the local market in minutes.

- **Headline:** Price like you can see the market
- **CTA:** Get Started
